ABOUT ME

Hi there, thanks for stopping by. This is my personal page so let me share with you a bit about myself! My name is Andrew Wong and I'm a 4th year Chemical Engineering student, minoring in Management and Society. Over the years I've gathered a multitude of experiences in my journey of life which has built my skills and inspired me to pursue a career in Product Management. While my engineering background has given me a solid base in technical understanding, my unique collection of experiences has brought me from leading cross-functional teams in waste processing, to marketing and growing my university's first ever sparring club, to consulting an oil and gas giant on automation, and even engineering process improvements inside a nuclear reactor!

My humble beginnings

In Engineering and Project Management

I went into engineering because I was fascinated with how people could use abstract principles of math and science to create ingenious products that solved day-to-day challenges. My first professional internship in Project Management exposed me the cross-functional teams that worked together towards billion dollar projects, from design, to engineering, to operations, to senior management.

Building my business acumen

This inspired me to learn more about the business side of things, where I took project-based courses in entrepreneurship and business strategy (I developed a mobile app), did some pro bono consulting Canada's largest oil & gas company, and even took home 1st place in my school's premier Management Consulting case competition with my amazing team of engineering students against business and MBA students.

Transition into Tech

Around the same time, my interest tech led me to discover Product Management, which I found to be the perfect intersection between engineering, business, and user experience. I quickly realized that being Product Manager meant that I needed to better speak the language of software engineers, and I needed to expand further than my fundamentals of C/C++, VBA, and Matlab I learned in school. I took it upon myself to self-learn elements of computer science. Python, HTML/CSS, JavaScript, are among some of the languages I've become more familiar with. I've also explored tools used by Marketing and Design, such as Google Analytics for online data collection and Axure RP for website/mobile app wireframing.
