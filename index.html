ABOUT ME

Hi there, thanks for stopping by. You've stumbled upon my personal page so let me share with you a bit about myself! My name is Andrew and I'm a 4th year Chemical Engineering student, minoring in Management and Society. Through my journey of life I've gathered a multitude of experiences which has shaped and inspired me to pursue a career in Product Management. While my engineering background has given me a solid base in technical understanding, my unique collection of experiences has brought me from: (a) Leading a cross-functional team for a $1 million secret waste processing project, to (b) Marketing and growing my university's first ever sparring club, to (c) Consulting an oil and gas giant on data visualization automation, and even (d) Improving engineering processes inside a nuclear reactor.

My humble beginnings

I went into engineering because I was fascinated with it was possible to use abstract principles of math and science to create ingenious products that solved day-to-day challenges. Chemical Engineering taught me to think critically, understand processes from a big picture, and use math (lots of it) to find efficiencies

Gaining industry experience

My first professional internship in Project Management exposed me the cross-functional teams that worked together towards a billion dollar project, from design, to engineering, to operations, to senior management. It allowed me to appreciate the science of taking the lead, aligning stakeholders, and gaining practical technical expertise.

Pictured: Inside of a CANDU nuclear reactor

Building my business acumen

This inspired me to learn more about the business side of things, where I took project-based courses in entrepreneurship and business strategy (including a global C2C classifieds business), did some pro bono consulting for Canada's largest oil & gas company, and even took home 1st place in my school's premier Management Consulting case competition, a team of engineering students against business and MBA students.

Pictured: The winning team (me on right)

Transition to Tech

Around the same time, my interest in tech led me to Product Management, which I found to be the perfect intersect between engineering, business, and UX. I quickly realized as a PM I needed to better speak the language of software engineers, on top of my fundamentals of C/C++, VBA, and Matlab I used in school. To-date, I have self-taught myself Python, HTML/CSS, JavaScript, among others languages and libraries. I've also Marketing and Design tools; such as Google Analytics for data analytics and Axure RP for wireframing.
